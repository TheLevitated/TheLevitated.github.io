YYY

Curated by cwl, Hello Ape Eyes presented the work of three female artists; Susan Beattie, Kristina Pulejkova, and Charlotte Wendy Law. Questioning, with arms raised in the “Y”, each a semaphore, indulging in material exploration.

hello, say hello, say hello ape eyes...

Opened and closed by performance, Hello Ape Eyes incorporated animation, site-specific sculpture, and sound. Embracing the uncertainties of today, the in-between and hybrid state of the artworks on show were of those that remained open to transformation. Between the glitched gaze of (mis)communication, continually deconstructing its own internal logic, and the sexualized earth laced with icing sugar, finding form and their feet, were alchemical relics; fragments captured as moments collapsing, infusing the shaft with the sounds of absence. For the private view Charlotte Wendy Law performed as part of an on-going collaboration with sound artist Artur Vidal, in an attempt to break, reform, and ultimately escape language through assemblage and sound. This low-fi physicality turned into the high-tech for the closing event. When, once again exploring forms of communication, Kristina Pulejkova drew from historical signs and signals to perform an experimental audio-visual set in collaboration with musician Glen Johnson.
