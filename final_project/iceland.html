she had a way of jumping

Two weeks in semi-isolation saw a return to stillness.
Time spent drawing, walking, making, resulted in multiple failed attempts at action: solitary performances given to an epic, wilful and fragile land. Bombastic, and beautifully bruised, Iceland became personified as the living-dead love child of John Carpenter and Sappho, Bardot, Monroe...

Yes, there was something special about me, and I knew what it was. I was the kind of girl they found dead in a hall bedroom with an empty bottle of sleeping pills in her hand.

The immensity and palpability of a sublime beauty caught up in its own corrosion and continual disintegration, held by the constant wind in rapture at time and the incremental processes it unfurled, resonated through pure matter as intimacy in motion, and spoke of tenderness.
The ground gave way tenderly.
Recording Iceland I recorded the feminine.
Immersed, images, sound, and film works developed alongside daily performances.

Past self-in-site research has taken place in; abandoned quarries, disused research labs of Oxford University, constructed scrape metal sets, and basements.
